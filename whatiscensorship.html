What Is Censorship?

Censorship

Censorship is the suppression or prohibition of words, images, or ideas that are considered to be "offensive" (What Is Censorship?). There are two forms of censorship which are:

  • Preventive - exercised before the expression is made public
  • Punitive - exercised after the expression is made public

What Is Censored:

  • Speech
  • Art
  • Books
  • Plays
  • Internet
  • Films
  • Radio
  • News reports
  • Periodicals
  • Television
  • Photography

Who Does The Censoring:

  • Governments
  • Churches
  • Private Groups
  • Speakers, Writers and Artists (self-censorship)
  • Anyone
